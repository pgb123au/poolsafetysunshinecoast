Why Maroochydore

The Maroochydore pool safety story.

The Maroochydore mix: CBD plus established family stock.

Maroochydore is more layered than most Sunshine Coast suburbs. The new Maroochydore CBD precinct (around the Sunshine Plaza and the Aerodrome Road corridor) carries low-rise apartments and strata townhouses. Around Cotton Tree, Picnic Point and the Maroochy River mouth you get established family homes from the 1980s onwards, many with in-ground concrete pools added in the 1990s and 2000s. The CBD end leans body-corp / shared-pool work (1-year Form 23). The Cotton Tree end is mostly private residential (2-year Form 23 if you live there, 1-year if you lease it).

Common Maroochydore failure: NCZ inside the fence line.

About 65% of Maroochydore first-up inspections fail on at least one item. The most common single failure is a climbable object inside the 900mm non-climbable zone — usually a pot plant, a built-in BBQ, or an air-con condenser placed when the pool fence was upgraded and the original landscape wasn’t re-thought. Easy to fix (move the object), painful to discover at settlement week. Worth walking the boundary before you book.

Body-corp + strata work.

Maroochydore CBD strata complexes commonly have a single shared rooftop or podium pool covering 20–80 units. These are shared pools under QLD law — 1-year Form 23, body-corporate responsibility. We work direct with strata managers on annual renewal scheduling so the cert never lapses between renewals. Multi-pool complexes (separate body-corp + recreation pool) are inspected together with a small per-additional-pool discount.

Working with Maroochydore conveyancers.

We’re happy to send the Form 23 (or Form 26 non-compliance notice) direct to your conveyancer or solicitor. Most Maroochydore residential settlements include the pool certificate as a special condition — getting our copy direct avoids the email-the-PDF-twice loop.
