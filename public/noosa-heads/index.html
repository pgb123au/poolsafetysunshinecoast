Why Noosa Heads

The Noosa Heads pool safety story.

Noosa Council, not Sunshine Coast Council.

Noosa de-amalgamated from Sunshine Coast Regional Council in 2014 and has run as a separate LGA ever since. For Form 23 purposes the QLD-wide rules (Building Act Pt 13, QDC MP 3.4, AS 1926.1) apply identically — same inspector requirement, same certificate, same Pool Safety Register. Where Noosa Council differs is in its local short-stay accommodation regulations and its planning scheme for premium-zone properties, which affects renovation work but not the inspection itself.

Hastings Street + Little Cove holiday lets.

Hastings Street and the immediate Little Cove + Park Road precinct carry one of Australia’s highest concentrations of premium short-stay accommodation. These pools are shared pools under QLD law — Form 23 valid for 12 months. The pool stock is generally well-designed (frameless glass, premium hardware) but the short-stay use pattern means guest furniture and decor routinely drift into the NCZ, gate latches get worn faster, and pool covers get left near the fence. Most Noosa Heads hosts schedule annual re-inspection one calendar month before expiry to avoid listing-down risk during the Christmas / Easter peak.

Premium private pools = bigger, more complex barrier work.

Noosa Heads private residential pools (Little Cove, Noosa Hill, Wyuna Drive, the upper Hastings Street headland) trend bigger, more architectural, often integrated into terraced landscaping with glass-balustrade boundaries that double as pool fences. These require more inspection time (45–75 min on site vs the standard 30–45) to walk the full barrier perimeter, check every spigot, every gate, every door from the dwelling into the pool zone, and every potential NCZ encroachment. We quote these at the higher end of the standard band ($240–$320) given the complexity.

Sunshine Beach + Castaways Beach.

South of Noosa Heads proper, Sunshine Beach and Castaways carry established 1980s–2010s residential homes plus a steady mix of holiday lets. Common failures here are the same as Caloundra — salt-corroded gate hardware, CPR sign weathering, NCZ creep from established gardens. Salt-air corridor is intense from Castaways through Sunshine Beach.

Typical Noosa Heads jobs.

  • Pre-sale inspection on a premium Little Cove home with frameless glass barrier ($240–$320)
